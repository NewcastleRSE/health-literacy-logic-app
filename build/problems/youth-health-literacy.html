Youth health literacy

Children and young people leaving school with low health literacy

Individual / community
Who should be involved?
  • Teachers
  • Schools
  • Local Councils
  • Libraries
  • Parents
  • Community Centres
  • Places of Worship
  • Nurseries
  • 'Hidden' care givers (such as relatives)
What are the causes of the problem?
  • Poverty
  • Education system that is not working properly
  • Teaching curriculum that is too full
  • Carers that no-one knows about e.g. relatives
  • lack of teaching about health and illness
  • Children and young people not feeling part of society
What would help?
  • Health literacy in teacher training
  • Health literacy in social work training (see also problem)
  • Teaching health literacy in schools
What actions are involved in doing something about this?
  • Staff training courses
  • Frequency of staff training
  • Reading groups
  • Engaging with local councils
  • Engaging with senior managers in schools
  • Mapping out children and young peoples' teaching organisations
How will we deliver these actions?
  • *Setting up children and young peoples' reading groups
  • *Training courses online and / or face to face
What do we expect to come out of these actions?
  • Skills to read for health
  • Skills to count for health
  • Skills to manage money
  • Children and young people having better health and well-being
  • Children and young people leading healthier lives
  • Healthier social interactions
  • Number of staff trained
  • Systems to map and monitor action plans
  • understanding levels of need
How will we know things have changed?
  • Reading counting and speaking skills for health will be better
  • More staff will have been trained
  • More action plans will be in place in places where children and young people learn
What short term changes will we see?
  • Mapping out of institutions and places to be trained
  • Training courses planned
  • Understanding levels of need
What do we think will happen in the longer term (3 years)? For example, increased health literacy, better disease control, improved health
  • Improved health literacy in children and young people
  • Healthier lifestyle - exercise, diet, smoking, alcohol
  • Increased health and well being
  • Healthier social interactions
  • Staff trained in health literacy
